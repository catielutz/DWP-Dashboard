Teen Pregnancy Dashboard

Teen Birth Rates Per Capita

This choropleth map visualizes the average teen birth rate per 1,000 females age 15-19 for each state from 2003 to 2018. The highest concentration of birth rates are found in the South of the United States, whereas the lowest birth rates are concentrated in Northeast. Explore the map in detail and predict the teen birth rate by county.

Publicly Funded Clinics Per County

This choropleth map displays the number of publicly funded health clinics available per county in 2010 and 2015. In the 5 years between 2010 and 2015, 2,300 new clinics were opened nationally, with the greatest number of openings in the state of California (612 statewide; 145 in Los Angeles) and closures in Iowa (17). Explore the map in detail and predict the teen birth rate by county.

Teen Birth Rate by Age Range

This visualization charts the teen birth rates from 2003 to 2018, separated by ages 15-17 and 18-19. The birth rate among teens aged 18-19 years is considerably higher than that of teens aged 15-17 years. This reflects that, despite a proliferation of media focused on young mothers, a majority of teen mothers are older than 18 when they give birth.

Birth Rate Trends by State and County

This visualization charts the change in the birth rate per 1,000 teens aged 15-19 from 2003 to 2018, organized by state in the top chart and county in the bottom chart. States and counties with the least and greatest amount of change will be pre-selected for any entered state. Although most counties reflect the overall trend of declining teen birth rates across the US, that change is not true of every county. This reveals that the factors contributing to the declining birth rate may not be equally present from county to county.
